# Defense Criteria Guide

## 1. Problem Definition & Objectives

### What This Criterion Means

This checks if the project has a clear problem, a valid purpose, and objectives that match the actual system developed.

### What The IT Panelists Will Look For

- Is the problem clear and real?
- Is it relevant to the IT field?
- Do the objectives match the system?
- Did the group build what the study promised?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our project addresses the limitation of traditional LMS platforms by developing an adaptive learning management system that identifies student learning preferences and transforms learning materials into more personalized content.”

## 2. System Implementation & Functionality

### What This Criterion Means

This checks if the system actually works, if the important features are complete, if the parts are connected properly, and if the system is usable and reliable.

### What The IT Panelists Will Look For

- Are the core features complete?
- Do the modules work together properly?
- Is the system reliable during actual use?
- Is it easy enough for users to use?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our system implements the core functions of a learning management system while integrating adaptive features that work together to deliver personalized learning content in a usable and reliable platform.”

## 3. Innovation / Technical Contribution

### What This Criterion Means

This checks what makes the project technically different, useful, and not just a basic LMS.

### What The IT Panelists Will Look For

- Is the solution new or improved?
- Are the technologies used appropriate?
- Does the system give a real technical contribution to the problem?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our project goes beyond a traditional LMS by combining learning style identification, machine learning, and AI-based content transformation to create a more adaptive and personalized learning experience.”

## 4. Documentation Quality

### What This Criterion Means

This checks if the paper and technical documentation are organized, complete, clear, and consistent.

### What The IT Panelists Will Look For

- Is the documentation well-organized?
- Are the important parts complete?
- Is the explanation clear and easy to follow?
- Is the content consistent with the actual system?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our documentation clearly presents the problem, objectives, methodology, system design, implementation, and results in an organized and complete manner.”

### Important Methodology Note

One current risk is that the methodology may describe what was used, but not clearly how it was actually done from start to finish.

## 5. Presentation & Demonstration

### What This Criterion Means

This checks how clearly the group explains the project and how effectively the system is demonstrated.

### What The IT Panelists Will Look For

- Is the presentation clear and organized?
- Is the demo smooth and easy to follow?
- Does the demo prove the important features?
- Can the group explain the technical parts while presenting?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our presentation and demonstration are designed to clearly show the problem, the solution, the system workflow, and the key adaptive features of the platform in a clear and understandable way.”

### Technical Parts Means

Technical parts are the important system and development details behind the project.

Examples:

- how the LMS works
- how FSLSM is used
- how XGBoost identifies learning style
- how Gemini AI transforms content
- how data is collected from user behavior
- how the database and modules connect
- how the system was tested and evaluated

### Demo Reminder

During the demo, do not only show that a feature works. Briefly explain what happens behind it in simple terms.

## 6. Response to Questions (Technical Defense)

### What This Criterion Means

This checks how well the group can defend the project when the panel asks deeper technical questions.

### What The IT Panelists Will Look For

- Can you justify your design decisions?
- Can you explain the technical details clearly?
- Can you defend why your solution is appropriate?
- Can you answer confidently and directly?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our group is prepared to justify the system’s design, explain its technical implementation, and defend why the chosen technologies and methods fit the goals of the project.”

### Example Questions

- Why did you choose FSLSM?
- Why did you use XGBoost instead of another model?
- How does the system decide what content to transform?
- How is user behavior collected and used?
- Why did you use Gemini AI?
- How do you know the model is reliable?

### Example Answer Style

“We chose XGBoost because it is effective for structured data, works well for classification tasks, and can handle complex behavior-based patterns needed for learning style identification.”

## 7. Research Design & Methodology

### What This Criterion Means

This checks if the group used the right research approach and followed a clear process in conducting the study.

### What The Statistician Will Look For

- Is the research design appropriate for the project?
- Is the sampling or respondent selection reasonable?
- Are the data gathering methods clear?
- Is the process of development, testing, and evaluation explained properly?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our study used an appropriate research design and a clear methodology to guide the system’s development, data gathering, testing, and evaluation.”

### Simple Meaning

`Research design` means the overall plan of the study.

It includes how the group evaluated the system, how data was gathered, who the respondents were, and how the objectives were tested.

If the study uses pre-test and post-test to compare the adaptive LMS with a traditional LMS like Google Classroom, that is part of the research design.

### If Asked “What Is Your Research Design?”

“Our research design is a pre-test and post-test based evaluation of the adaptive LMS, supported by system assessment and statistical analysis.”

### Model Evaluation Note

These are part of methodology and model evaluation under the research design:

- R² score and MAE from regression evaluation
- K-Fold Cross Validation
- ROC curves with AUC scores across all four dimensions
- Training data with ILS-validated ground truth labels from the eye-tracking study

### Simple Meaning Of The Checks

`Is the sampling or respondent selection reasonable?`
Did the group choose the right participants, and is there a clear reason why those people were included?

`Are the data gathering methods clear?`
Did the paper clearly explain how data was collected, such as surveys, pre-test/post-test, system logs, questionnaires, or user evaluation?

`Is the process of development, testing, and evaluation explained properly?`
Did the study clearly explain how the system was built, how it was tested, and how the results were measured?

## 8. Statistical Analysis

### What This Criterion Means

This checks if the group used the correct statistical tools and analysis methods for the study.

### What The Statistician Will Look For

- Were the right statistical tools used?
- Was the data processed properly?
- Were the analysis techniques appropriate for the objectives?
- Were the results computed and presented correctly?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our study used appropriate statistical tools and analysis methods to evaluate the system’s performance, effectiveness, and research results.”

### Example

If the study used the following, these are part of the statistical analysis:

- pre-test and post-test scores
- mean or average
- Likert scale results
- FURPS evaluation
- model metrics like R², MAE, ROC, and AUC

### Simple Example Answer

“Our statistical analysis included comparing pre-test and post-test results, computing mean scores from respondent evaluations, and using model evaluation metrics to assess the accuracy and reliability of the machine learning component.”

### Purpose Of Statistical Analysis

The purpose of statistical analysis is to turn collected data into evidence.

It helps prove whether the system is effective, whether the results are meaningful, and whether the conclusions are supported by numbers instead of opinion only.

## 9. Interpretation Of Results

### What This Criterion Means

This means explaining what the results actually mean.

### What The Statistician Will Look For

- Did the group explain the results correctly?
- Did the group connect the results to the objectives?
- Did the group avoid overclaiming?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our results were interpreted based on the objectives of the study to determine whether the system was effective, functional, and aligned with the intended outcomes.”

## 10. Validity & Reliability Of Data

### What This Criterion Means

This checks if the data is trustworthy and consistent.

`Validity` means the study is measuring what it is supposed to measure.

`Reliability` means the results are consistent and dependable.

### What The Statistician Will Look For

- Was the data collection credible?
- Were the instruments or measures validated?
- Are the results dependable enough to support the conclusions?

### Strong Simple Line

“Our study ensured that the collected data was credible, relevant, and consistent enough to support valid and reliable conclusions.”

## 11. Individual Performance Evaluation Criteria

### Knowledge Of The Project

This means each member must understand the whole project, not just their own part.

### Individual Technical Contribution

This means each member should clearly explain what specific part they personally handled.

### Ability To Answer Questions

This checks if the member can answer clearly, correctly, and confidently.

### Problem-Solving & Critical Thinking

This checks if the member can justify decisions, explain issues, and suggest improvements.

### Communication & Presentation Skills

This means speaking clearly, organizing ideas well, and explaining in a professional way.

### Professionalism & Preparedness

This checks confidence, readiness, behavior, and teamwork during the defense.
